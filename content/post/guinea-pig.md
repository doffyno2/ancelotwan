<h2>Introduction</h2>
<p>Welcome to our comprehensive guide on guinea pigs! Guinea pigs are cute, cuddly, and make great pets. They are also known as cavies and are native to South America. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know about guinea pigs, including their history, behavior, diet, health, and more.</p>

<h2>History of Guinea Pigs</h2>
<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Guinea%20Pig%20History&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Guinea Pig History" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>
<p>Guinea pigs were first domesticated in South America more than 5000 years ago. They were originally kept for their meat and fur, but their gentle nature soon made them popular as pets. In the 16th century, European explorers brought guinea pigs to Europe and they quickly became popular as pets. Today, guinea pigs are popular pets all over the world.</p>

<h2>Behavior of Guinea Pigs</h2>
<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Guinea%20Pig%20Behavior&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Guinea Pig Behavior" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>
<p>Guinea pigs are social animals and thrive in pairs or small groups. They are active during the day and sleep at night. Guinea pigs are curious and playful and love to explore their surroundings. They are also vocal animals and communicate with each other through a variety of sounds, including squeaks, chirps, and purrs.</p>

<h2>Diet of Guinea Pigs</h2>
<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Guinea%20Pig%20Diet&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Guinea Pig Diet" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>
<p>Guinea pigs are herbivores and require a diet that is high in fiber. Their diet should consist of hay, fresh vegetables, and a small amount of pellets. It is important not to overfeed guinea pigs as they are prone to obesity. Fresh water should also be available at all times.</p>

<h2>Housing and Environment</h2>
<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Guinea%20Pig%20Housing&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Guinea Pig Housing" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>
<p>Guinea pigs should be housed in a large cage with plenty of space to move around. The cage should be lined with bedding that is absorbent and easy to clean. Guinea pigs also need access to fresh air and natural light. It is important to provide them with toys and activities to keep them mentally stimulated.</p>

<h2>Health and Care</h2>
<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Guinea%20Pig%20Health&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Guinea Pig Health" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>
<p>Guinea pigs require regular veterinary care to ensure they remain healthy. They should be examined by a veterinarian at least once a year. Guinea pigs are susceptible to a variety of health problems, including dental problems, obesity, and respiratory infections. It is important to provide them with a healthy diet, clean living environment, and plenty of exercise to help prevent these issues.</p>

<h2>FAQs</h2>

<h3>1. How long do guinea pigs live?</h3>
<p>Guinea pigs can live up to 8 years with proper care and attention.</p>

<h3>2. Can guinea pigs be housed with other pets?</h3>
<p>No, guinea pigs should not be housed with other pets as they may be seen as prey animals.</p>

<h3>3. How often should I clean my guinea pig's cage?</h3>
<p>You should clean your guinea pig's cage at least once a week, and spot clean as needed.</p>

<h3>4. What should I do if my guinea pig stops eating?</h3>
<p>If your guinea pig stops eating, it is important to seek veterinary care immediately as this can be a sign of a serious health problem.</p>

<h3>5. Can guinea pigs be litter trained?</h3>
<p>Yes, guinea pigs can be litter trained with patience and consistency.</p>

<h3>6. What should I do if my guinea pig gets sick?</h3>
<p>If your guinea pig gets sick, it is important to seek veterinary care immediately.</p>

<h3>7. How often should I trim my guinea pig's nails?</h3>
<p>You should trim your guinea pig's nails every 4-6 weeks.</p>

<h3>8. Can guinea pigs be trained?</h3>
<p>Yes, guinea pigs can be trained with patience and positive reinforcement.</p>

<h3>9. Do guinea pigs need to be bathed?</h3>
<p>No, guinea pigs do not need to be bathed as they are able to groom themselves.</p>

<h3>10. What should I do if my guinea pig is shedding excessively?</h3>
<p>If your guinea pig is shedding excessively, it is important to seek veterinary care as this can be a sign of a health problem.</p>
