<h2>What is a PET scan?</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Pet%20Scan&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Pet Scan" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>PET stands for positron emission tomography. It's a type of imaging test that uses a radioactive tracer to detect certain conditions in your body. During a PET scan, you'll lie down on a table that moves through a large circular machine. This machine detects the tracer and creates images of your internal organs and tissues.</p>

<h2>When is a PET scan necessary?</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Pet%20Scan&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Pet Scan" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>A PET scan can be used to diagnose a variety of conditions, including cancer, heart disease, and neurological disorders. It can also be used to monitor the progression of certain diseases and to evaluate the effectiveness of treatment.</p>

<h2>How do I prepare for a PET scan?</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Pet%20Scan&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Pet Scan" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Before your PET scan, you'll need to fast for several hours. You may also be asked to avoid certain medications and to abstain from strenuous exercise. Be sure to follow all of your doctor's instructions carefully to ensure accurate test results.</p>

<h2>What happens during a PET scan?</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Pet%20Scan&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Pet Scan" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>During your PET scan, you'll lie down on a table that moves through the circular machine. You'll need to remain still for the duration of the test, which typically lasts between 30 and 90 minutes. Once the scan is complete, you'll be free to go home.</p>

<h2>Are there any risks associated with a PET scan?</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Pet%20Scan&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Pet Scan" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>While a PET scan does involve exposure to a small amount of radiation, the risk of harm is minimal. However, if you are pregnant or breastfeeding, you should inform your doctor before undergoing a PET scan.</p>

<h2>How do I interpret the results of a PET scan?</h2>

<center>
	<img src="https://tse1.mm.bing.net/th?q=Pet%20Scan&w=800&h=500&c=1&rs=1" alt="Pet Scan" width="800" height="500" style="display: block; width: 100%; height: auto">
</center>

<p>Your doctor will interpret the results of your PET scan and use them to diagnose or monitor your condition. The images produced by the scan will show areas of your body where the tracer has accumulated. These areas may indicate the presence of disease or may show how well your body is responding to treatment.</p>
